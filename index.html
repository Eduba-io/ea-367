01 / The Eduba frame

Most small hardware teams automate the wrong things.

A working view of a dual-use drone company. About 60 percent of what happens every day is traditional database and operations work. About 30 percent is rule-based logic. Around 10 percent is a real AI problem. The work is sorting those before anything gets built.

For Xpece that means a separate operational lane for the defense motion (NDAA-compliant BOM, past-performance capture, Blue UAS path, ITAR scope), with the consumer DTC channel running beside it on the existing Shopify and Amazon infrastructure. Two lanes, one knowledge layer.

The two lanes share who fielded what, who tested what, and which supplier is cleared for which program. They share nothing else. That separation is the sub-brand decision. It is the first conversation we would like to have.

60 / 30 / 10 as it maps to Xpece
  1. 60%Database & code
    SKU catalog. BOM tracking. RMA ticketing. Dealer terms.
  2. 30%Rule-based logic
    Warranty triage. Export-control checks. Pricing tiers.
  3. 10%Genuine AI
    Flight anomaly detection. Autonomy on a dual-use variant.
